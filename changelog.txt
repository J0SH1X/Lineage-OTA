    vendor image.

commit 5b4ea6a41086b8ccbfd3db48572019d0cb91a643
Author: Danny Lin <danny@kdrag0n.dev>
Date:   Mon Feb 1 18:12:06 2021 +0800

    sdm845-common: overlay: Don't pin camera app in memory
    
    The default camera app can be *huge* in some cases, e.g. when the app in
    question is Google Camera. The system will only pin up to the first 80
    MiB of the APK file, as well as the first 80 MiB of its odex. There are
    several problems with this:
    
      - We could easily end up with 160 MiB of camera app files pinned,
        which is a tall order with the usable RAM that we have
      - The data that gets pinned may not even be the most critical data for
        launching the camera
    
    Let's disable pinning of the camera app to save precious RAM on this
    device.
    
    Change-Id: I745e39b6ff212dfaf9213517d2a2be8ff7384e24
    Signed-off-by: AnierinB <anierin@evolution-x.org>

    updatable_apex.mk to avoid being overwritten.

    sdm845-common: Restrict aux cameras only to OnePlus Camera
    
    On enchilada and fajita, the 2nd back camera is used only for
    calculating depth data for things like bokeh mode. In normal operation,
    users should not be able to use the 2nd back camera directly.
    
    With that in mind, it should be noted that four cameras are exposed.
    Cameras 0 and 1 are main back and front respectively. Camera 2 is the
    2nd back camera. Camera 3 is a virtual camera which shows camera 0's
    feed, but applies bokeh effects using data from camera 0 and 2 in
    realtime.
    
    Given the information above, the only apps that should have access to
    cameras 2 and 3 are OnePlus's camera apps, since they'd actually utilize
    said camera feeds for things like bokeh mode. It would not make sense to
    give other apps access to these cameras, especially when some of them
    end up having issues trying to open said cameras.
    
    (org.codeaurora.snapcam is still included mostly to stop complaints from
    gcam users--the 2nd back camera is still pointless and wouldn't bring
    anything new to gcam.)
